Vietnamese Egg Roll recipe

History

Vietnamese egg rolls, Chả Giò are a product of Vietnam being under the rule of China. Vietnam was under the rule of China for many centuries, the Vietnamese egg rolls hold similarity to the
Chinese ones. Chả Giò were origanally made for royalty which explains why they are also called "Imperial Rolls" however, they are called different names from different places (like summer rolls,
fried spring rolls, etc.). Chả Giò is what people from Southern Vietnam call it. It is an important dish of Vietnamese culture and is used in many festivities.

This recipe is my grandmas which she taught my mom which will eventually be passed down to my sisters and I.

Ingredients

  • 1 lbs of taro
  • 2-3 pcs of carrots
  • 2-3 pcs of onions
  • 2pcs of bean thread vermicelli
  • 1/2 lbs of mung bean
  • 1 package of crab meat 8oz
  • 2 tbsp of salt
  • 1 tbsp of msg
  • 2 tbsp of chicken flavoring
  • 2 packages of egg roll wrappers
  • 2 tbsp of tapioca powder

Instructions

  1. Shred the taro and carrots
  2. Cut the onions
  3. Cut the bean thread vermicelli to smaller strands
  4. Mix all the ingredients together in a large mixing bowl
    (taro, carrot, onions, vermicelli, mung beans, and crab meat)
  5. Add in all the seasonings
  6. Mix well and set aside
  7. Mix tapioca powder with some water and microwave for 1 minute
    to make a sticky component to bind the rolls
  8. Start wrapping

Frying Instructions

  1. Prepare oil in a frying pan
  2. Add in egg rolls and fry until golden brown
  3. Remove them from the oil
  4. Let it cool and then serve
